The Role of Isostasy in the Evolution of Fold and Thrust Belts

Here we explore the role of the load imposed by the stacking of thrust sheets on the structure of fold and thrust belts. We explore the interplay between the isostatic compensation rate and the rate of tectonic loading.

Exploring the Mechanical Stratigraphy of the Papuan Fold Belt

This pilot study demonstrates that numerical experiments can deliver realistic sets of structures commonly observed in fold and thrust belts. Using the Papuan Fold Belt as an example, we aim to model the structural styles that result from the local mechanical stratigraphy. This approach is potentially useful for resource exploration as it provides a technique for predicting subsurface trap geometries within fold and thrust belts. Ultimately, classifying the mechanical stratigraphy of fold and thrust belts with heterogeneous sedimentary layering is key to understanding their evolution, subsurface geometry and optimising exploration efforts. This work was done as part of my Honours thesis in 2019.

The Influence of Mechanical Stratigraphy on the Structural Style of FTB's

Here, we investigate how the mechanical properties of rocks control the structural style of fold and thrust belts. Mechanical stratigraphy refers to the mechanical layering present in a stratigraphic column, involving the succession of competent and less competent lithologies. For a given set of boundary conditions and amount of shortening, the mechanical stratigraphy is expected to influence the partitioning of shortening between folds and thrusts, their respective distribution pattern, their respective wavelengths, and possibly the nature and expression of their interaction. This project was part of my Honours thesis in 2019.
