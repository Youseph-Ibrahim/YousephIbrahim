About Me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate based in Sydney, researching the evolution of fold and thrust belts using a combination of numerical models and fieldwork.

I was born in Sydney, but left at the young age of 1. I grew up constantly moving countries, and lived in New Zealand, Taiwan, Slovenia, the United Arab Emirates, Kuwait and Jordan, before relocating back to Australia for University.

I've always been interested in natural history and the Earth. So pursuing a double major in Geology/Geophysics and Biology seemed natural. Throughout my undergraduate I developed a keen interest in structural geology, and so I went on to do an honours degree in fold-and-thrust belt, followed by the Ph.D I'm in now.

Outside of the Ph.D. I still like to enjoy my time outdoors through running, bushwalking, rock climbing, and photography (shameless Flickr plug https://flic.kr/ps/3QFJVj).
